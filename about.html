About

Building an evidence-based scientific position on precautionary management of jumbo flying squid (Dosidicus gigas) within SPRFMO.

Three lines of action

Given the complexity of the decision process and the technical disagreements between the squid models, three non-exclusive lines of action are proposed. Each aims to give Scientific Committee members information that complements — rather than replaces — the population models currently in play.

Line 1 · Under way Multi-model risk analysis Weights population projections from a set of models (Peru, Chile, China) under differing assumptions about fishing effort. Its defining feature is the capacity to incorporate the views of a broad group of stakeholders, and to weight the credibility of each projection on data, empirical evidence and expert judgement.

Line 2 · Proposed Empirical Management Strategy Evaluation Applies the technical and methodological basis of a standard MSE to test, by simulation, how different models respond to alternative harvest control rules. Models that lack robustness show little sensitivity across control rules — and can therefore be set aside. No results yet — this line is proposed, not started.

Line 3 · Committed Refining the environment-driven assessment Delivers the commitments made in SC13-Obs03: widening the fishing units (fleets) represented, and capturing differences in natural mortality between zones.

How the three fit together

The three lines answer different questions, and are deliberately not alternatives to one another:

Question it answers Relationship to the models
Multi-model risk analysis Given that the models disagree, what can we still say with confidence? Carries all three models as weighted hypotheses
Empirical MSE Which models are robust enough to inform a decision? Tests the models against harvest control rules
Model improvement Can the remaining objections to the environment-driven assessment be closed out? Improves one model

Line 1 is the most developed: it is set out in full in the working paper, which defines risk following FAO guidance as “the probability of something undesirable happening”, and shows how competing hypotheses are hierarchized, weighted, and combined into probability statements that can seed a harvest control rule.

Implementation

The immediate objective is to develop at least one of these lines far enough to present an implementation scheme — structured along the lines of terms of reference — to the 14th Meeting of the SPRFMO Scientific Committee (SC14, September 2026).

The lines differ in complexity and in the level of technical dedication they demand. For SC14 the realistic aim is therefore to set out the basis for implementation schematically, rather than to deliver all three in full.

The work is organised around three steps:

Step 1 · Before the meeting Technical document Preparation of the technical document, together with the supporting material used to present it — agreed with the counterpart before submission.

Step 2 · During Representation at SC14 Scientific representation at the meeting of the SPRFMO Scientific Committee, September 2026.

Step 3 · After Post-meeting review A retrospective assessment of how the position was received, used to identify and scope the next round of technical actions.

A detailed explanation of the methodological schemes behind each line of action can be provided and discussed on request.
